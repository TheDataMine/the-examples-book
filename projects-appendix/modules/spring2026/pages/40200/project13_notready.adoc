== Project Objectives
This project introduces you to working with local large language models (LLMs) using Ollama. You will learn how to run models, compare their outputs, evaluate their limitations, and customize their behavior through prompting.

.Learning Objectives
****
- Run and interact with local LLMs using Ollama  
- Download and compare multiple models (e.g., `llama3.2` and `mistral`)  
- Measure and evaluate model performance and response quality  
- Identify hallucinations in model outputs and explain their implications  
- Use external data sources (e.g., PDFs, vector databases) to enhance model responses  
- Modify system prompts to control tone, style, and personality of model outputs

The sky appears blue because of a phenomenon called scattering, which occurs when sunlight interacts with the tiny molecules of gases in 
the Earth's atmosphere. Here's a simplified explanation:

1. Sunlight enters the Earth's atmosphere and consists of a spectrum of colors, including all the colors of the visible light.
2. When sunlight travels through the atmosphere, it encounters tiny molecules of gases such as nitrogen (N2) and oxygen (O2). These 
molecules scatter the light in all directions.
3. The shorter wavelengths of light, such as blue and violet, are scattered more than the longer wavelengths, like red and orange. This 
is known as Rayleigh scattering, named after the British physicist Lord Rayleigh, who first described the phenomenon.
4. As a result of this scattering, the blue light is distributed throughout the atmosphere, reaching our eyes from all directions.
5. Our eyes perceive this scattered blue light as the color of the sky, which appears blue to us during the daytime.

It's worth noting that:

* During sunrise and sunset, the sky can appear reddish or orange due to a different scattering effect called Mie scattering, which 
occurs when light interacts with larger particles in the atmosphere, such as dust and water droplets.
* At night, the sky appears dark because the sun is not emitting any light.
* The color of the sky can also be affected by atmospheric conditions, such as pollution, dust, or weather patterns.

In summary, the sky appears blue due to the scattering of sunlight by tiny molecules in the atmosphere, which preferentially scatters 
shorter wavelengths of light, like blue and violet.
----
